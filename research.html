RESEARCH

Galactic Archaeology is a field dedicated to reconstructing the formation and evolution of the Milky Way by examining the current properties of its stars. Stars preserve in their atmospheres the chemical signatures of the gas clouds from which they formed. As different generations of stars end their lives, they enrich the interstellar medium with new chemical elements, creating a record of Galactic history. By analyzing these chemical properties alongside kinematic data, it is possible to distinguish between distinct stellar populations and trace the processes that shaped them. Because we can observe it with a high level of detail from the inside out, the Milky Way serves as a primary laboratory for studying disk galaxies. My research focuses mostly on the Galactic disk. Using chemical and kinematic analyses, I investigate the mechanisms involved in the formation and evolution of the Milky Way’s structures, with a particular emphasis on the thin and thick disk configuration.
